Featured Painter

Johannes Vermeer

The Master of Light. Johannes Vermeer (1632–1675) created only about 34 paintings in his lifetime, yet he remains one of the most revered artists of the Dutch Golden Age. Working slowly and with immense care in his Delft studio, Vermeer transformed everyday domestic scenes — a woman pouring milk, a girl reading a letter — into timeless meditations on stillness and light. His brilliant use of ultramarine and mastery of optical effects continue to baffle and inspire artists today.

Key Works: Girl with a Pearl Earring • The Milkmaid • View of Delft

Museum of the Month

The Prado Museum, Madrid

Widely considered to have the single greatest collection of European painting in the world, the Museo Nacional del Prado is not an encyclopedic museum like the Louvre. Instead, it is a collection of kings. Built on the passionate tastes of the Spanish monarchy, it boasts unparalleled holdings of Velázquez, Goya, Bosch, and Titian. Walking its halls is like reading the personal diaries of the Habsburg and Bourbon dynasties.

📍 Address: Calle de Ruiz de Alarcón 23, Madrid
🕒 Hours: Tuesday–Sunday, 10:00–20:00
🎟️ Tickets: €15 general admission (free Mon–Sat 18:00–20:00 & Sun 17:00–19:00)
